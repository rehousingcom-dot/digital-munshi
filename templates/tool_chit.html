How a committee (BC) works

A committee (also called BC, chit fund or kitty) is a group savings scheme. For example, 10 people put in money for 10 months to build a pool. Every month there is a bid (boli) — whoever needs the money bids a discount (a "loss" they accept). The highest bidder takes the pool minus their bid, and that bid amount is shared among all members as a dividend, lowering everyone's payment that month.

What is the interest rate?

Whoever takes the money early is effectively borrowing: the discount (bid) they gave up is the cost of getting the money early. This calculator estimates that as an approximate annual interest rate = discount ÷ amount received × (12 ÷ months) × 100. Whoever takes the pool last earns the shared dividends instead. Figures are indicative; actual returns depend on each month's bid.
