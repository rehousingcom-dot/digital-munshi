How a committee (BC / chit) works

A committee is a group savings scheme — for example 12 members put in money for 12 months to build a pool (say ₹6,00,000). Each month there is a bid (boli); the winner takes the pool minus their bid (the "net payable"). Because of the discount, all members pay a little less that month. Whoever takes early accepts a bigger discount (they effectively borrow and pay interest); whoever takes last pays the least and earns the most.

Net payable and interest

Net payable = total amount − bid. Each member's contribution that month = net payable ÷ members. Your gain or cost = what you receive minus what you pay in total. The effective interest shown is an approximate annualised figure for your chosen month.
